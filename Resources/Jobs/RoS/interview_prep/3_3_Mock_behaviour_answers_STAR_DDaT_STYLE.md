For **UK public-sector architecture roles** (like the **Database Technical Architect at Registers of Scotland**) the behavioural questions tend to follow the **DDaT competency style**. They are less about niche tech and more about **risk, governance, resilience, and leadership**.

If you walk into that interview ready for the **7 questions below**, you will be prepared for ~80% of what they could ask.

---

# The 7 Questions UK Government Architects Almost Always Get Asked

## 1️⃣ Leading Technical Change

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time you led a significant technical change or transformation.”

What they’re testing:

* Strategic thinking
* Architecture ownership
* Managing complexity

Good examples:

* Cloud migration
* Platform redesign
* Introducing infrastructure as code
* Replatforming databases

Strong answers mention:

* **NFRs**
* **risk mitigation**
* **stakeholder alignment**
* **measurable outcome**

---

# 2️⃣ Managing Risk

Public sector organisations care **a lot** about risk.

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time you identified or managed a significant technical risk.”

They want to see:

* Risk identification
* Mitigation strategy
* Calm response

Examples:

* data loss risk
* replication failure
* security vulnerability
* backup failure
* architectural fragility

Mention:

* monitoring
* mitigation
* governance

---

# 3️⃣ Influencing Stakeholders

Architects rarely have direct authority.

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time you influenced a technical decision.”

They’re testing:

* persuasion
* communication
* diplomacy

Examples:

* pushing back on unsafe architecture
* negotiating cost vs resilience
* convincing teams to adopt standards

Strong answers include:

* business framing
* cost vs impact
* compromise solutions

---

# 4️⃣ Handling Ambiguity

Architects often work with **incomplete information**.

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.”

They want to see:

* structured thinking
* risk awareness
* iterative decisions

Example answers:

* designing architecture before all requirements were known
* choosing a platform with evolving workloads

Mention:

* assumptions
* risk management
* iterative approach

---

# 5️⃣ Learning from Failure

This is almost guaranteed.

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time something didn’t go to plan.”

They want:

* honesty
* accountability
* learning

Weak candidates blame others.

Strong candidates show:

* ownership
* reflection
* improvement

Example:

* migration issue
* upgrade regression
* incident response

---

# 6️⃣ Collaboration Across Teams

Public sector is very **cross-team heavy**.

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time you worked with multiple teams to deliver a solution.”

They want to see:

* collaboration
* communication
* leadership without authority

Examples:

* migration projects
* platform changes
* incident recovery

---

# 7️⃣ Creating Technical Standards or Strategy

This is a **classic architect question**.

**Question**

> “Tell us about a time you introduced standards or a technical strategy.”

They want:

* strategic thinking
* long-term planning
* architectural governance

Examples:

* standardising database platforms
* monitoring standards
* infrastructure as code frameworks
* security baselines

---

# What They Are Actually Scoring

The panel usually scores answers against competencies like:

| Competency           | What they want           |
| -------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Technical leadership | guiding architecture     |
| Risk management      | protecting services      |
| Communication        | explaining complex ideas |
| Collaboration        | working across teams     |
| Strategic thinking   | long-term improvement    |

---

# A Trick That Makes Candidates Score Higher

When answering, **mention these concepts explicitly**:

* **Resilience**
* **Security**
* **Cost optimisation**
* **Scalability**
* **Operational supportability**

Those are classic **architectural NFRs**.

Example phrase:

> “Before choosing the solution I considered the non-functional requirements — resilience, scalability, operational supportability and cost.”

Interviewers love hearing that.

These appear surprisingly often.

### ❓ “How do you ensure architecture remains aligned with business goals?”

and

### ❓ “How do you prevent technical debt building up?”

Prepare short answers for both.
