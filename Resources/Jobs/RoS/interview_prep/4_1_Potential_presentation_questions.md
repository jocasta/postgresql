# The Questions They Will Almost Certainly Ask

The presentation itself is usually straightforward.

The **real test is the questions afterwards**.

Expect these.

---

# Question 1

**“What are the biggest risks in your design?”**

They want to see:

* realism
* risk awareness

Good answer includes:

* migration risk
* vendor lock-in
* operational capability
* cost growth

Never say **“there are no risks.”**

---

# Question 2

**“How would you manage the migration of critical services?”**

They are looking for:

* incremental migration
* replication strategies
* rollback capability

Mention:

* pilot migrations
* blue/green
* phased rollout

---

# Question 3

**“How would you ensure resilience?”**

Mention:

* multi-AZ architecture
* automated failover
* backups
* DR testing
* monitoring

---

# Question 4

**“How do you balance cost against resilience?”**

Very common question.

Good answer:

* understand service criticality
* apply different resilience tiers
* optimise infrastructure sizing
* monitor utilisation

This shows **architect-level thinking**.

---

# Question 5

**“How would you reduce operational burden on the database team?”**

Mention:

* managed services
* automation
* infrastructure as code
* standardisation

---

# Question 6

**“How would you ensure security and compliance?”**

Mention:

* encryption
* access control
* auditing
* security reviews
* alignment with organisational standards

---

# Question 7

**“How would you measure success?”**

This is a very good architect question.

Possible metrics:

* system availability
* deployment frequency
* incident reduction
* infrastructure cost
* platform adoption

---

# Question 8

**“What would you do first in your first 90 days?”**

Panels love this.

Strong answer structure:

1. understand current estate
2. engage with teams
3. assess risks and technical debt
4. define architectural roadmap

---

# Question 9

**“What trade-offs did you make in your design?”**

They want to hear:

> architecture is about balancing trade-offs.

Examples:

* cost vs resilience
* flexibility vs standardisation
* speed vs governance

---

# Question 10

**“How would you ensure teams follow your architecture?”**

Key idea:

Architects **influence**, they don’t dictate.

Mention:

* standards
* architecture reviews
* collaboration
* documentation

---

# The Single Most Important Thing to Show

Panels want to see **this mindset**:

> The role of architecture is to enable teams to deliver reliable services while managing risk, cost and technical debt.

If you consistently frame answers around **risk, value, and delivery**, you will sound like a **C1 architect** rather than a DBA.
