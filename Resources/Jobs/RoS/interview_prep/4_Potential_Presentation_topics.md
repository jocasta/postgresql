Given the **RoS job description**, the **C1 architect level**, and typical **UK public-sector DDaT interviews**, the case study will almost certainly fall into one of a few predictable themes.

They won’t test obscure database tricks. They will test:

* architecture thinking
* risk management
* migration strategy
* governance
* cost/value judgement

Below are the **most likely presentation scenarios** and the **questions they typically follow with**.

---

# Highly Likely Presentation Topics

## 1️⃣ Migrating Legacy Databases to Cloud

This is **the most likely scenario**.

Example prompt:

> “Registers of Scotland operates several legacy on-premise databases supporting critical services.
> Design an architecture to migrate these systems to a modern cloud platform while ensuring security, resilience and minimal disruption.”

What they want to hear:

* migration strategy
* risk management
* phased approach
* cloud managed services
* governance

Things you should mention:

* discovery and assessment
* lift-and-shift vs re-platform
* Aurora / RDS type services
* replication for migration
* rollback plans
* DR strategy

---

## 2️⃣ Designing a Resilient National Service Platform

Very common for government roles.

Example prompt:

> “Design a resilient data platform that supports high availability digital services used by citizens and businesses.”

They are testing:

* availability
* disaster recovery
* scaling
* observability

Key themes:

* multi-AZ / multi-region
* backup strategy
* failover
* monitoring
* incident response

---

## 3️⃣ Reducing Technical Debt in a Database Estate

Extremely aligned to the RoS advert.

Example prompt:

> “RoS currently operates a large database estate with increasing technical debt.
> Propose an approach to modernise the platform while minimising risk to live services.”

They want to see:

* pragmatic thinking
* incremental improvement
* governance

Mention:

* platform standardisation
* automation
* managed services
* retirement of legacy tech

---

## 4️⃣ Designing a Secure Data Platform

Possible because government roles emphasise security.

Example prompt:

> “Design a secure architecture for managing sensitive land and property data.”

They will look for:

* encryption
* IAM controls
* network segmentation
* auditing

Mention:

* encryption at rest / in transit
* least privilege
* audit logging
* compliance controls

---

## 5️⃣ Enabling Faster Delivery for Product Teams

Very DDaT-style question.

Example prompt:

> “Product teams are struggling to deliver new services due to infrastructure constraints.
> Design a database platform that improves delivery speed while maintaining reliability.”

Focus on:

* self-service infrastructure
* automation
* CI/CD
* platform engineering

Mention:

* infrastructure as code
* automated provisioning
* standardised database platforms
