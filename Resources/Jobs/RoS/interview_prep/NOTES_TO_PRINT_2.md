# Technical Architect Case Study – Presentation Notes

## 1. Context & Objectives

* What problem are we solving?
* Who are the users / services?
* What is the business outcome?

Focus on:

* Reliability of national services
* Scalability
* Cost efficiency
* Reduction of technical debt
* Faster delivery for product teams

---

## 2. Assumptions & Constraints

Clarify early.

Possible assumptions:

* Mixed legacy estate (on-prem + cloud)
* Multiple database technologies
* Existing technical debt
* Limited operational capacity

Constraints:

* Security & compliance
* Data integrity
* Budget limits
* High availability requirements
* Public sector governance

---

## 3. Current Risks / Challenges

Typical issues:

* Ageing infrastructure
* Operational complexity
* Manual provisioning
* Limited observability
* Performance bottlenecks
* Scaling limitations
* Cost inefficiencies

---

## 4. Architecture Options

Always present **2–3 options**.

Example structure:

Option A — Rehost / lift-and-shift
Option B — Managed cloud services
Option C — Platform approach with automation

Purpose:

* Show decision thinking
* Demonstrate trade-off analysis

---

## 5. Trade-offs

Compare options across:

* Cost
* Risk
* Technical debt
* Operational complexity
* Scalability
* Vendor lock-in

Key message:

> Architecture is managing trade-offs, not finding perfect solutions.

---

## 6. Recommended Approach

Explain **why this option is best**.

Focus on:

* Reliability
* Maintainability
* Operational efficiency
* Alignment with cloud-first strategy
* Long-term sustainability

Avoid deep technical detail.

---

## 7. Phased Roadmap

Architects show **how to deliver change safely**.

Example phases:

1. Assess current estate
2. Pilot migration
3. Incremental service migration
4. Platform standardisation
5. Optimisation and cost management

---

## 8. Governance & Assurance

Important in government environments.

Mention:

* Architecture review
* Security review
* NFR validation
* Cost monitoring
* Operational monitoring
* Documentation and standards

---

# Non-Functional Requirements (Always Mention)

These are extremely important.

* Availability / resilience
* Performance
* Scalability
* Security
* Observability
* Disaster recovery
* Cost efficiency

---

# Key Phrases to Use

These signal senior architecture thinking.

* “Managing technical debt”
* “Balancing trade-offs”
* “Incremental delivery”
* “Reducing operational burden”
* “Enabling product teams”
* “Designing for resilience”
* “Cloud-first but pragmatic”

---

# If You Get Stuck During Questions

Pause and structure your answer:

1. Clarify assumptions
2. Identify risks
3. Outline options
4. Explain trade-offs
5. Give recommendation

---

# Final Closing Statement

Finish with something like:

> “The goal of this architecture is to provide a resilient and scalable platform that supports Registers of Scotland’s digital services while reducing operational complexity and enabling teams to deliver value faster.”
